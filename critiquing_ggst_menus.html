Critiquing Guilty Gear Strive's Menus

While I have no issues with the art style of the menus in Guilty Gear -Strive-, I have numerous problems with their functionality.  Changing the organization of the menus would improve player experience as game modes would be contained in a more logical manner.


As seen in the above image, a player repeatedly pressing “A”  (or “X” on PlayStation controllers) would cause them to enter the arcade mode. While arcade modes are usually the primary single-player mode in fighting games, it doesn’t make sense for this to be the game mode that requires the minimum amount of effort to navigate to. I feel this way because most fighting game players spend the majority of their time in training mode or battles between two players, so one of those options should be the simplest mode to navigate to.

Another gripe with the menu layout is that Player vs. CPU battles are separate from local battles between players. As seen below, there is a separate menu option for 2-Player battles that has no submenus:

Instead, there should just be a “local battle” option. If only one controller selects to play, a CPU automatically becomes the player’s opponent. This would reduce the number of menu options and potential confusion when navigating.

The organization of the “Dojo” category could also benefit from rearranging its options:

It appears that the submenu options were organized in this way so that new players would first complete the tutorial, then the missions mode, and finally enter training mode. The tutorial is extremely bare bones, as it teaches the buttons and nothing else, while the missions mode gives an in-depth explanation of the combo system, system mechanics, as well as other gameplay elements. While this option certainly benefits players new to the genre, this inconveniences veteran players as well as former new players. Although a minor inconvenience, placing training mode at the end of the “Dojo’s” menu requires several inputs to access a mode that makes up many players spend the majority of their play time in.


I would rework GGST’s menus so that a “Local Battle” option is at the top of the menu, allowing for one or two player battles. I would then place the “Dojo” submenu underneath this, and move training mode to be the leftmost option. While this may cause some players to experience training mode before the tutorial, it is a more beneficial design for long-term players.

Lastly, I would place the “1-Player” submenu at the bottom, with the vs. CPU option being reworked into the “Local Battle” option. While this buries single-player content outside of playing against CPUs, I feel like this is a smaller issue than those presented in the official layout.
