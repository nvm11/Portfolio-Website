RPS Rumble

The core gameplay of fighting games boils down to rock, paper, scissors. Generally speaking, attacking beats grabbing, grabbing beats blocking, and blocking beats attacking. While their core gameplay is simple in theory, I believe fighting games are one of the hardest genres of games to be "good" at. Unlike most types of video games, fighting games are a 1-on-1 competition between two players, with little to no luck involved. If you lose in a fighting game, it means your opponent has better decision making skills and execution; there is no one to blame but yourself, and the only way to improve is from losing, learning, and adapting. This cycle can be discouraging for new players, as it takes months or possibly years to get to a level of competency that veteran players have.


Background and Description:

RPS Rumble aims to take a simplistic approach to the fighting game genre. Rather than being traditional fighting game-styled gameplay, RPS Rumble is turn-based, allowing players a few seconds to contemplate which option they wish to select.

Gameplay:

The execution some fighting games are known for has been completely eliminated, replaced by simple inputs (forward for attack, back for block, and up for grab). While some may feel like simplifying fighting games to this degree would create a boring experience, I believe this will not be the case. Eliminating any form of execution outside of pressing one button allows inexperienced players to pick up the game and understand how to play relatively fast. Players of any skill level can experience the joy of picking the right option for a scenario, and can come to understand why their decisions were wrong in some scenarios, as there are no other elements of gameplay distracting them from their conclusion.

In-Game UI Concepts:

When designing the UI for the game, I kept in mind that it had to be intuitive enough so that any player could enjoy the game without going through a tutorial or reading any instructions.


The goal behind the layout above was to inform the player of their options as well as the inputs to perform them.

Similar to other fighting games, the health bars within RPS Rumble start out as green, and will deplete as they take damage. Additionally, as players’ health bars deplete, the color of their health bar changes to reinforce that their health is in a critical state.

There is also a round clock. Rather than being a clock that counts down from A specific number and results in a time out when it is depleted, the time is how long players have to make their next decision.

Experimental Gameplay Elements:

Supers:

If you noticed earlier, there was a super meter near the health bar. Seeing as RPS Rumble strays from the traditional fighting game genre, I’m not sure that supers would mesh well with the gameplay. The current concept I have for them is that when your super meter is full, it will affect how your next action functions. For instance, if you choose attack, your attack is doubled, if you choose grab, your grab will beat attack, or if you choose defense, you won’t take chip damage for the next few turns. While I feel like this mechanic would add complexity to the game, I also don’t want to stray too far from creating a simple to understand game.

Taunting:

When reading the gameplay and in-game UI sections, you might have been asking “what occurs if the player doesn’t press anything?” I feel like this would be a good space to implement taunting into the game. I am currently unsure of how this action will affect gameplay, maybe a successful taunt has the same effects as supers, but less severe, or maybe they will charge the super meter? Regardless of how they affect gameplay, I feel like including them would provide characters with a sense of personality.
