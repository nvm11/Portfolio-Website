Why Street Fighter 6 has the Best UI in its Genre

After being the lucky few who were selected for the Street Fighter 6 Closed Beta, I was blown away by numerous aspects of the game. I enjoyed the gameplay and artstyle of the game, but found the UI within rounds to be the most impressive aspect. The placement of the “Drive Gauge” drastically improves gameplay and decreases room for error, and is a revolutionary choice for fighting game UI/UX.

The drive gauge (circled in red) causes meter usage in Street Fighter 6 to be wildly different from any other game in the series. This gauge is utilized for ex moves, offensive mechanics, and defensive mechanics. The meter seen at the bottom of the screen is strictly used for supers (“Critical Arts” in Street Fighter). This choice allows the super meter to be looked at less often, as I found that supers were used once or twice a game at most. However, the drive gauge is used on both offense and defense very frequently. While the gauge is not very stylistic, its placement drastically improves its functionality, and makes other games’ user interfaces seem obsolete in comparison.

When landing a combo, there are three separate locations for important information. Street Fighter 6’s UI solves this common issue among fighting games by having important information in one location. I felt that this one change drastically improved my decision making within matches, as I was able to observe all the necessary information to make informed decisions.
